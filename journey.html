My Journey So Far

I was unfortunately made redundant from my previous role as a customer service assistant back in September 2023, I was with the company for 7 fun years. I had the pleasure of making some good friends while I was there, but unfortunately the company went under.

Much of my working career has been in retail, but I want to change this because of the attitudes which were seen in the recent past, my unfortunate redundancy and also my health. I decided that enough was enough and I turned to look to a brighter future, which presents new challenges.

My new focus was to gain employment in tech, but I wasn't too sure of the direction I wanted to go. At first I thought I'd try my luck and see if I could just step into some random IT role. I had already acquired NVQ's for both Information Technology and Business Administration after I had left school. But I soon found that it wasn't going to be that simple... But I do like a challenge though.


The Challenge: Update my skills.

After nearly 6 frustrating months without gaining employment in any I.T. based industry. I was presented with an opportunity to improve my chances by going on an intensive course:


Course 1: City & Guilds Level 2 Diploma in ICT Systems Support.

  • Service Provision – Distinction.
  • Install, Configure and Test ICT Networks – Distinction.
  • Principles and Concepts of Cloud Computing – Distinction.
  • Concepts for IT Professionals – Distinction.
  • IT – Fundamentals – Networking.
  • IT – Fundamentals – Software.
  • IT – Fundamentals – Hardware.

This was the first of my courses for 2024. There was a lot of information to pack into just two months. I found it enjoyable, especially building a few systems within a budget. But this isn't new to me as I've built and upgraded systems before. On the plus side at least I can say that I can build, maintain and upgrade systems officially now.

I had learned a few new things in regards to building systems, especially concerning the Power Supply Unit (PSU). Because I had taught myself, I hadn't recognised that it is advised that you add 20% to the total power output required to cater for possible future upgrades.

As the course progressed, I had to rewrite my entire CV and include one of these titles, depending on the job I am applying for. Honestly that seemed strange, but they explained that doing so would encourage employers to notice a level of confidence.
Also, according to the tutors, on completion of this course, it should opened the doors for entry level positions for:

  • IT Technician
  • IT Support Technician
  • Digital Support Analyst
  • IT Helpdesk Support
  • 1st and 2nd Line Support Agent
  • IT Service Engineer
  • Cloud Services Technician
  • Junior System Administrator

    • I then tried again, having at least some direction I could go in. I was still struggling to obain employment in IT. My Advisor at the Jobcenter tried to get me to be more specific and nail down where I'd like to be in IT. It took a few chats, but I looked towards Data Analyst. They then pointed me in the direction of a coding course, as this was a vital part of a Data Analyst role. I began to look at what coding courses would entail. Each one pointed to having basic knowledge of Python.

    Ok, a new challenge presented itself. Me being me thought "Ok if that's what it takes, I'll do it". So I looked to W3Schools to gain some knowledge on the basics.


    Course 2: Level 2 NCFE Certificate in Understanding Coding

    • HTML Programming Language
    • CSS Programming Language

    This course wasn't quite what I was expecting, but I am definitely enjoying it. It doesn't have any focus on Python, though it been has touched upon it. The main focus of this course is the languages of HTML and CSS. Strangely though, I have found myself excited of a possible future in Web Development. It has inadvertantly turned my head away from Data Analyst roles. I think this maybe down to my creative nature.

    The HTML aspect of this course isn't entirely new to me, as in the early days of the internet, I was playing around with this language after a friend showed me a website that he had made using MS Word.
    His method was more around making tables and inseting images or text as needed, which worked and was the basis in where I started. Then I was curious about how it all worked. I decided to take a look at what was happening behind the scenes, and through trial and error, taught myself the basics... There was no Youtube or ChatGPT in the 90's and I didn't think courses even existed for this. I also know through doing this course that my method of coding in HTML was actually 'inline'. This method is to be avoided because it would cause problems with the resulting page... That explains a lot! I always had problems.

    HTML has changed since I had originally coded as a teen, not drastically, some things haven't really changed. But it has allowed me to get to grips with the new functions and additions since my early endevours. It has also allowed me to be able to work with a new language which I've never used before, CSS. It's a little tricky to get used to, but it's nothing I can't handle.
    I'm enjoying using both HTML and CSS (even though they get me banging my head against the desk at times). I find it fun discovering what both these languages can do when paired together. Such as making this very websites. It's a little on the basic side as I'm getting used to CSS.

    The roles I can apply for:

    • Front-end Web Developer (UI/UX)

    This course has reignited my interest in HTML and is encouring my creativity. This course is admittedly inspired me to pursue a career path in Web development. It has so far been an enjoyable course.
